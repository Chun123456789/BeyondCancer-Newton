A message from our President

Why I Started This Project

To start off, my dad's mother battled cancer and lost. It was a tough hit, especially for my dad — she raised him and my uncle alone, and the grief was undeniable. I was only one year old at the time, so I didn't understand much until later in life. I couldn't bear the pain my dad carried from losing someone so important to our family to cancer. That moment made everyone realise how ruthless cancer is. More than a decade has passed, and it feels like everyone has moved on except my dad. Every Mother's Day, I can still hear a faint sob from him. It made me tear up too. That's how fate is.

A few years ago, devastating news came again. My mom went through an x-ray scan and doctors found an abnormally large tumour. I remember the smell of the sterilised bedsheets she was lying on, and the voices of doctors discussing the next course of action. My vision blurred as the hospital lights shone through my eyes — cancer was back in our family, and it was threatening my mom. I asked myself, "has this been decided by fate?" Looking back, most of my mom's family members have also suffered from cancer, especially breast cancer. Some fought it and won; others weren't so fortunate.

That's when I realised: everyone prays for cancer to go away, but no one takes actual action. Right then, I promised myself to learn more about cancer. I know many other people have lived through something similar, and I believe there is a way to help prevent this pain from repeating in other families. That's why I want to volunteer, help underprivileged cancer patients facing near-impossible obstacles, and educate underprivileged children and communities to raise awareness. These goals are deeply personal to me — but I want to share them with everyone. This project is dedicated to spreading awareness of how dangerous cancer can be, and to doing something about it, together.

President, Beyond Cancer Newton
